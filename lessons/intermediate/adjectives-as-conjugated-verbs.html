Adjectives - Adjectives as Conjugated Verbs.

A high variety adjectives are made from conjugated verbs. These verbs may be present continuous or past simple.

Present Continuous (-ing).
Here's a list of the most frequent '-ing' adjectives:
Interesting
Amusing
Boring
Exciting
Dissapointing
 Surprising
Worrying
Frightening
Tiring
Annoying

For example, if you say something is dissapointing, it's because it dissapoints you.
  • That videogame was dissapointing...

    • If you say something is annoying, it's because it annoys you.
  • My neighbor's dog barked all night long. It was annoying.

    • Past Simple (-ed).
    Here's a list of the most frequent '-ed' adjectives:
    Interested
    Amused
    Bored
    Excited
    Dissapointed
    		 Surprised
    Worried
    Frightened
    Tired
    Annoyed

    For example, if something worries you, you can say you feel worried.
  • I'm worried about Tim. I haven't seen him in a week.

    • If something tires you, you can say you feel tired.
  • I'm feeling tired right now. I worked all day.
